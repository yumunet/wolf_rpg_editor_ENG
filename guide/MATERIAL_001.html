【Change Map Chip Passability Settings】

[Goal]
　Change the passability settings for map chips.
　Here, as a test, we will try to make the "water area (ocean, lake, or river)" in Sample Map A passable.

[1]
　Click the Tileset Settings icon .
[2]
　The window that appears is the "Tileset" window.
　This window is used to configure map chip settings.
　
　1. Select the tileset used in "Sample Map A".
　　　Confirm that "Town/Dungeon" is selected.
　
　2. Confirm that you are on the screen for configuring passability settings.
　　　Confirm that the "Passability Settings" button is pressed.
　
　3. The item circled as ○3 in the figure is the water area in "Sample Map A".
　　　It should currently be marked [×].
　　　
　　　Click the water area map chip several times to change it to [○].
　
　　　The order cycles: ○→×→▲→★→□→○...
　　　Right-clicking cycles in reverse order.
　
　4. When done, click the OK button.

　※Additional Info
　　With the Ver2.00 update, the passability settings interface changed.

　Regarding step 3 above:
　　In Ver2.00 and later, you first select the change method from [○/×], [★/▲], or [↓/□],
　　then click the target map chip.

　　Click the button you want to change from the buttons circled with ★ in the figure below,
　　then click the target map chip.

【Additional Info 1: Passability settings other than ○ and ×】

　In addition to "passable [○]" and "impassable [×]", passability settings include
　[▲], [★], and [□ (bottom half filled)].
　
　[▲]: Passable, and the chip is drawn above the character when the character moves behind it
　　　Map chips with this setting are drawn above the character "when the character goes behind them".
　　　("Displayed above" means "displayed overlapping the character".)
　　　"Stair handrails" and "upper parts of walls" have this setting.
　
　[★]: Passable, and always drawn above the character
　　　Map chips with this setting are drawn above the character "at all times".
　　　"Signs" and "tops of trees" have this setting.
　
　[□]: Standing on this chip makes the character's feet (16px) semi-transparent
　　　On chips with this setting, the character's feet become semi-transparent.
　　　It is commonly used for effects such as "grass on a field map" or "forests on the world map".
　　　It can also be used for "passable water areas".
　
【Additional Info 2: Difference between [▲] and [★]】
　
　　　Both are set when you want something drawn above the character,
　　　but there is a difference between "only when the character goes behind it" and "always".
　　　
　　　It's difficult to explain in words, so please look at the figure below.
　　　
　　　
　　　
　　　The left (stone tile) is set to [▲], and the right (wooden floor) is set to [★].
　　　Both are drawn on layer 2.
　　　
　　　Focus on the two female characters in the foreground.
　　　On the right [★], you can see that the upper part of the character is slightly hidden.
　　　
　　　On the other hand, on the left [▲], the female character in front of the map chip is fully visible,
　　　while Wolfar, who is behind the map chip, is hidden by the chip.
　　　
　　　This is the difference between [▲] and [★].
　　　
　　　As a general guideline for when to use each:
　　　[▲] for things placed on the ground, like handrails
　　　[★] for things high up, like signs
　　　— that's a good way to think about it.
　　　
　　　For fine details, just change them when something looks wrong during Test Play.
【Additional Info 3: Quarter-tile passability settings】

　To configure passability settings, left-click or right-click the map chip you want to configure,
　but if you hold [Shift] while clicking,
　you can configure passability settings in quarter-tile units.
　Each tile is divided into a 2×2 grid (like the character 田), and you configure whether each quarter is passable or not.
　
　Additionally, [Ctrl] + [Shift] + [left-click] sets a quarter tile with [▲] passability,
　and [Ctrl] + [Shift] + [right-click] sets a quarter tile with [★] passability.
　
　※ This has no effect unless the movement step is set to 0.5 tiles, so please take note.
