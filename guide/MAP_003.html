【Copy All 3 Map Layers at Once】

[Goal]

　When creating a map, you will frequently copy and paste parts.
Normally you can only copy one layer at a time, which can feel tedious.
This time, we will show you how to copy parts from all 3 layers at once.
For this tutorial, we will copy & paste the dungeon building in Sample Map A.

【Steps】

[1] First, open Sample Map A.

This time, using the building with a dungeon on the right side as an example,
we will try to copy this building (the building where Eddy is).

The roof and wall parts of this building are drawn on layer 1, the fence and stairs on layer 2,
and the door and fence edges on layer 3.
If you copy this building normally, you would need to copy & paste 3 times,
which is tedious and requires position alignment... but there is a better way.

(Do you remember that right-dragging allows you
to copy an entire area at once?)

[2] Copying all layers at once — the key is the CTRL key

To copy all 3 layers at once, the key technique is
right-drag while holding the CTRL key.
Be careful not to release the CTRL key midway — try copying the range shown in the figure to the right.

[3] Paste everything at once

Were you able to copy successfully?
The dotted outline should now move around in the shape of what you just copied.
Next, let's paste the copied data.

This time, let's paste it near the "farm guy" in the lower left
(sorry, farm guy).
To paste, move your mouse cursor to the top-left tile of the desired position
and left-click while holding the CTRL key.
Move the mouse cursor to the position shown in the figure to the right,
and click there...

[4] See the result

How does it look?
The building from before, including its walls, doors, and everything else, has been pasted in the selected location.

As long as you don't right-click somewhere else, the copied data is retained,
so you can paste it into additional locations as well.

Note: Only map chips can be pasted this way.
Events are NOT copied & pasted.

That's all for this tutorial. Thank you.
